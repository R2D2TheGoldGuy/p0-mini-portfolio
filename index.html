About The Creator

My name is Ken Chung, I am the blogger and content creator of Agoge Code. I’m currently working part-time, and also studying as an online student. My main course of study is the treehouse front-end techdegree program, I’m also taking a few self-paced courses on edX. My goal is to become a professional web developer; I plan on achieving this by building a portfolio, blog, YouTube channel, and having certifications on edx.

About The Project

Welcome to my mini portfolio, I call it my 'Mini Portfolio' because its basically like codepen.io, a playground for me to tinker with different types of technologies. I create small projects to challenge myself to try new things and build something creative. These projects are also intended to demonstrate my skills and knowledge as part of my portfolio, not to mention that they're also projects I develop for fun. Whenever I learn new concepts and technologies, I always make sure I can apply it to a small project, this way I can get the opportunity to practice my coding skills to learn something practical while doing hands-on work. These small projects are known as mini projects, and I've decided store all of my mini projects onto a second portfolio website.

Career and Purpose

I'm a big believer in self-teaching, I take the phrase "learning by doing" very seriously. my goal is to become a professional self-taught web developer. I'm currently trying to specialise in Front-End Development using technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JQuery, Bootstrap, SASS, React, and the MEAN Stack. I also want to slowly transition into full-stack web development in the future so that I can expand my skillset and keep learning new technologies to keep up with the industry. The backend languages that really interest me are C#, Python, and full stack JavaScript, I hope to get to that stage one day.
